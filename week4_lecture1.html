Week 4: Research Design & Causality (Flagship Lecture)

1. The central problem of policy analysis

Policy decisions require causal claims:
  • Did the program work?
  • Did the policy cause the outcome?
But most data only shows differences.

2. The classic mistake

Participants in a job program have higher employment rates.

Does this prove the program works?

3. Confounding variables

Confounders influence BOTH:
  • Participation
  • Outcome
Confounding diagram:
Motivation → Program Participation
Motivation → Employment

4. Research design hierarchy

  • True experiment (strongest)
  • Natural experiment
  • Observational study (weakest)

5. Why random assignment matters

Without random assignment:
Motivated → Treatment
Less motivated → Control
With random assignment:
Motivation distributed evenly

Which isolates causation?

6. Internal validity

Internal validity asks: Did the treatment actually cause the outcome?

7. Threats (MRS SMITH)

  • Maturation
  • Regression
  • Selection
  • History

8. Apply a threat

Students improve after a year. Why?

9. External validity

Even if causal:
  • Does it generalize?
  • Does it work elsewhere?

10. Final synthesis

To evaluate a policy claim, ask:
  • Was there random assignment?
  • Are there confounders?
  • Is internal validity strong?
